At a crossroads

This week, I updated my catalogue of making, created a small publication, and reflected on the summative feedback for my dissertation draft. This reflection presented a crossroads filled with doubts, uncertainties, and insecurities. Ultimately, I did not reach a decision. If this CPJ were a TV show, this week would have ended with a corny cliffhanger HAHA.

  • This week, the atelier had a surprise visit from the Dean, Nur, and we were asked to present our process. In preparation, I created a small zine that showcases the stories I learned through experimentation. The zine follows my CPJ and aims to guide the user through my experiments.

  • Reflection: Presenting my zine to the class was quite overwhelming as I have a lot to share, which mostly were stories of my discoveries. And through this zine, I realise storytelling is more than just being honest with my thoughts and wild imagination, there should be a point to the story - and right now the point is very vague and lack specifics. But this struggle is part of the process, I make it happen first and then make it good later. The goal is vague also because I have not decided specificially what kind of human and nature relationship I am refering to. Furthermore, what environmental degradation am I refering too? Is it climate change, water pollution or soil, air pollution?

I feel genuinely lost stuck right now because it's either I come up with a sketch that reimgaines how human can sense the natural environment (which means possibly forgoing my mini experimentations) or I reframe my research to something more relevant to what I have been making.
